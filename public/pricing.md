# The Azizi Firm Pricing and Fee Model

## Short answer

The Azizi Firm generally handles employment law, personal injury, auto fraud, wrongful death, and lemon law matters on a contingency fee basis.

- No consultation fee for an initial case review.
- No hourly billing for most plaintiff-side matters.
- No attorney fee unless there is a recovery through settlement, verdict, buyback, replacement, or another financial resolution.

## What "contingency fee" means

A contingency fee means the attorney fee is tied to the outcome of the case rather than billed by the hour. If there is no recovery, there is generally no attorney fee owed for the work performed under that arrangement.

## Typical case categories handled this way

- California employment law claims, including wrongful termination, discrimination, retaliation, harassment, wage and hour disputes, leave violations, and whistleblower matters.
- Personal injury claims, including car accidents and wrongful death cases.
- Lemon law claims involving defective vehicles under warranty.
- Auto fraud claims involving dealer misrepresentation or concealment.

## What can affect the fee structure

The exact contingency percentage and treatment of litigation costs depend on the engagement agreement and the facts of the case. Relevant factors may include:

- Whether the case resolves before a lawsuit is filed.
- Whether the matter proceeds through litigation, arbitration, mediation, or trial.
- Whether a statute allows fee shifting, as in some lemon law or consumer cases.
- The scope of expert work, records collection, discovery, and motion practice required.

## Case costs and expenses

Cases may involve costs such as filing fees, deposition expenses, record retrieval, expert review, mediation fees, or service costs. How those expenses are handled should be confirmed in the written fee agreement.

## Timing expectations

Every matter is different, but many cases follow a pattern like this:

1. Initial consultation and case screening.
2. Evidence review and strategy assessment.
3. Pre-suit demand, agency filing, or both, depending on the claim.
4. Negotiation, mediation, or litigation.
5. Resolution through settlement, verdict, buyback, replacement, or dismissal.

Simple matters may resolve faster. Cases involving litigation, expert discovery, multiple parties, or disputed damages usually take longer.
